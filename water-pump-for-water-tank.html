19. 1. Word Problem: Pump A can fill a tank of water in 5 hours. Pump B can fill the same tank in 8 hours. How long does it take the two pumps working together to fill the tank?(round your answer to the nearest minute).- what is given?- what is asked?- solution- final answer​


Answer:

Given:

Pump A can fill a tank of water in 5 hours.

Pump B can fill the same tank in 8 hours.

Asked: How long does it take the two pumps working together to fill the tank?

Solution:

•The combined flow rate of the two pumps is the sum of the flow rates of each individual pump.

•The flow rate of Pump A is 1/5 tanks per hour and the flow rate of Pump B is 1/8 tanks per hour.

•Therefore, the combined flow rate of the two pumps is (1/5) + (1/8) = 1/4 tanks per hour.

•It will take 1/4 * 4 hours = 1 hour for the two pumps working together to fill the tank.

•There are 60 minutes in an hour, so it will take the two pumps working together 1 * 60 = 60 minutes to fill the tank.

Final answer:

It will take the two pumps working together 60 minutes to fill the tank.
