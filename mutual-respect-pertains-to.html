22. Explain intersubjective by using the following keywords;A. Mutual respectB. Genuine communicationC. To be is to be withD. DecenteringE. Human living​


A. MUTUAL RESPECT

Mutual respect is the basis for the acceptance that the other must occupy a space as part of our reality. In this sense, mutual respect is manifested in attitudes such as the ability to listen, speak moderately and tolerate the position of others with respect to our position.

Mutual respect in intersubjective relationships refers to the understanding that individuals in a relationship will show respect and consideration for one another's thoughts, feelings, and opinions. This means that each person in the relationship will listen to and consider the other's perspective, and will communicate in a way that is non-judgmental and non-critical. Mutual respect is an important foundation for any healthy and fulfilling relationship, whether it is a romantic partnership, a friendship, or a professional collaboration.

B. GENUINE COMMUNICATION

Genuine communication means speaking, active listening, understanding, and action.

Genuine communication in intersubjective relationships refers to open, honest, and authentic communication between individuals. This means that each person in the relationship is willing to share their thoughts, feelings, and opinions with the other, and is open to listening to and considering the other's perspective. Genuine communication allows individuals to understand each other better, and can help to build trust and deepen the relationship. It is an essential component of any healthy and fulfilling intersubjective relationship.

C. TO BE IS TO BE WITH

The phrase of "to be is to be with" simply means -one has to accept what is real and what is reality surrounding him or her.

The phrase "to be is to be with" in an intersubjective situation means that an individual's identity and sense of self is closely tied to the relationships they have with others. In other words, an individual's relationships with others play a significant role in defining who they are and how they see themselves. This is because, as social beings, humans have a natural tendency to form relationships with others, and these relationships can have a profound impact on an individual's sense of self. Therefore, being in relationships with others is an essential aspect of what it means to be human.

D. DECENTERING

Decentered intersubjectivity is a key element in the process of self-understanding, whereby the self-knowledge of emotional experience is prone to inaccuracies that develop through an imperfect understanding of the generalized other.

Decentering in an intersubjective situation refers to the process of stepping back from one's own perspective and attempting to understand and empathize with the perspective of others. This means that an individual will try to see a situation from the other person's point of view, rather than only considering their own perspective. Decentering is an important skill in intersubjective relationships, as it allows individuals to better understand and communicate with one another. It can also help to reduce conflict and increase trust and intimacy in the relationship.

E. HUMAN LIVING

It is seen as a term to describe the complicated interpersonal and relational matrix that human beings happen in from birth and a necessary constituent of subjective experience.

Humans living in an intersubjective refers to the way that humans interact and relate to one another in their daily lives. This includes the relationships and connections that individuals have with others, as well as the ways that they communicate and understand each other.

Human living is an essential aspect of the human experience, as humans are social beings who naturally form relationships with others. These relationships can provide support, companionship, and a sense of belonging, which are all important for human well-being. Therefore, human living in an intersubjective is an essential part of what it means to be human.

23. What is intersubjectivity by using mutual respect?


What is intersubjectivity?

It is the shared concept, idea, or view between two or more people. Intersubjectivity shows that even though individuals are unique and have their unique experiences, they can still share or agree on a subject.

Let us take a look at the next question for an example.

How intersubjectivity related to respect?

Another way of seeing intersubjectivity in relation to respect is that by keeping an open mind and accepting that people will always be different from one another due to their own experiences and learned behavior, people can still get along by agreeing to respect one another and agreeing to disagree on certain things.

Since there are more than thousands of different cultures in the world, the definition and views surrounding respect may differ from one person to another.

A Filipino child may be expected to look straight into his parents' eyes as a way to show respect and sincerity while a Japanese child is taught that looking straight into the eyes of an elder is disrespectful and a show of scorn. These two acts are opposites but are considered acceptable in their own cultures.

If we take away the act, we are left with the concept of respect. In both cultures, they do agree that respect is due to elders and parents. So while they both have a different way of doing things, they still have something in agreement. This is the same for many other things in the world.
