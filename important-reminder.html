14. What important story does the belen remind us about

Answer:

(see explanation)

Explanation:

The belen, or nativity scene of the birth of Jesus Christ has a lot of elements, most of which relate to who Jesus is and who Jesus is supposed to be.

First, we recall first why Joseph and Mary went to Bethlehem. Augustus Ceasar ordered a census of all of Rome and it required everyone to go to their town to register. Being a descendant of King David, Joseph and the then pregnant Mary went to Bethlehem. Eventually Mary gave birth to Jesus in Bethlehem, fulfilling a prophecy from the prophet Micah about a ruler coming from Bethlehem (Micah 5).

In the belen scene, there is a star of Bethlehem, which guided the three wise men to where Jesus was born. The star is also a prophecy about a ruler coming out from a star from Jacob's bloodline (Numbers 24:17). King David is from Jacob's bloodline, which makes Joseph of the same bloodline.

The prophet Isaiah (Isaiah 7:14) also had a prophecy about the Lord giving a sign and a child being conceived by a virgin. In this story, it refers to Mary giving birth to Jesus.

These prophecies hint the people that Jesus is the fulfill-er of the Lord's promise; He is the chosen on by the Lord that will deliver His people from the Romans.

Moving on to other elements, the belen took place in a manger or a stable, a place where animals are kept. Since there was a massive movement of people due to the census, inns were full and there were no available place for the couple to stay and spend the night. Despite being the promised One, God, and Jesus did not use their "godly" powers to force others to let them in an inn, or a mansion "befitting" the holy and high nature of someone to be named King, or ruler. This is a message of humility; despite their ability to exhibit extraordinary things, they chose to stay on what is available to them.

It also sends a message that Jesus will rule over all creation and not just humans; which is why they were in a manger surrounded by animals and other creations and not in an inn surrounded by people. Jesus did not have a grand entrance in this world despite being the ruler of all.

With these elements, Jesus was not your typical ruler or king that would rule using force but rather a different approach. He would not satisfy people's earthly wishes (to be freed from the Romans), but that He would give spiritual guidance to everyone, as what the Israelites have missed for a long time.
