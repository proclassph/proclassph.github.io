24. 1/10 of the Tom's marbles are all pink. 4/9 of the remaining are striped marbles. The rest of the marbles are crystal clear. If Tom has 30 crystal marbles in his urn, how many more crystal marbles than striped marbles does Tom have?


Crystal comparison the marbles of the striped marble that Tom has is 4 : 5.

Step-by-step explanation:

Be discovered:

1/10 of the Tom's marbles are all pink.

4/9 of the remaining are striped marbles.

The rest of the marbles are crystal clear.

Tom has 30 crystal marbles in his urn.

Question:

How many more crystal marbles than striped marbles does Tom have?

Asked:

The first step is to find the number of parts of Tom's marble that are not red.

[tex]\frac{1}{10}[/tex] of the Tom's marbles are all pink.

Marbles are not red = [tex]\frac{10}{10} -\frac{1}{10} =\frac{9}{10}[/tex]

The second step is to find the sum of the parts of the striped marble from all the pieces.

[tex]\frac{4}{9}[/tex] of the remaining are striped marbles.

The striped part of the marble = [tex]\frac{4}{9} *\frac{9}{10} =\frac{4}{10}[/tex].

The third step, we look for the number of clear marbles or crystals.

The rest of the marbles are crystal clear.

Part of a crystal marble = [tex]\frac{10}{10} -\frac{1}{10} -\frac{4}{10} =\frac{5}{10}[/tex]

The fourth step, we look for the comparison of crystal marbles with lined marbles.

The ratio of the crystal marbles to the striped marbles = [tex]\frac{4}{10} :\frac{5}{10} = 4 : 5[/tex]
