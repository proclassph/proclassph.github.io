8. What are the similarities and differences between the Sherlock Holmes collection and The Dopple Ganger Chronicles?​


Answer:

Similarities between Holmes and Watson in HOUN are that Watson gets to act as a detective for a change: not only in the opening scene with the walking-stick, but when he accompanies Sir Henry to Dartmoor (leaving Holmes in London, or so he thinks), and is trying to figure out what's going on (as in his interviews with Laura Lyons and Mr. Frankland). Watson also gets to be a man of action, as his friend often is: investigating the light on the moor, and going to visit the hut on the moor.

Differences? Watson is probably more sympathetic to those around him than Holmes would be; in the original stories one gets the sense of Watson being the voice of calm and reason while Holmes goes off on a scent. And of course a major difference is that Holmes had, during his independent investigation after arriving in Dartmoor, already arrived at the identity of the murderer while Watson was still uncertain.
