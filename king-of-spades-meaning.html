Answer:

a. P(king) = 4/52 = 1/13

b. P(face card) = 12/52 = 3/13

c. P(red card) = 26/52 = 1/2

d. P(ace) = 4/52 = 1/13

e. P(2 or jack) = P(2) + P(jack) - P(2 and jack) = 4/52 + 4/52 - 1/52 = 7/52

f. P(heart or king of spades) = P(heart) + P(king of spades) - P(heart and king of spades) = 13/52 + 1/52 - 1/52 = 13/52 = 1/4

g. P(red or black card) = P(red) + P(black) - P(red and black) = 1/2 + 1/2 - 0 = 1

h. P(black card or queen of hearts) = P(black card) + P(queen of hearts) - P(black card and queen of hearts) = 26/52 + 1/52 - 0 = 1/2

i. P(ace or clubs) = P(ace) + P(clubs) - P(ace and clubs) = 4/52 + 13/52 - 1/52 = 4/13

j. P(face card or diamond) = P(face card) + P(diamond) - P(face card and diamond) = 12/52 + 13/52 - 1/52 = 24/52 = 6/13

k. P(10 or red card) = P(10) + P(red card) - P(10 and red card) = 4/52 + 26/52 - 2/52 = 28/52 = 7/13

l. P(number card or spade) = P(number card) + P(spaded card) - P(number and spaded card) = 40/52 + 13/52 - 10/52 = 43/52

15. find the probability of drawing the king of spades from a complete deck of cards​


Answer:

1/52

Explanation:

P(A) = n(A)/n(S)

P(A) is the probability of an event “A”

n(A) is the number of favourable outcomes.

n(S) is the total number of events in the sample space.

There are 4 kings, but only one King of spades in a deck of cards, and 52 total number of cards (with joker) or 48 total number of cards (without joker).

Using the formula,

P(A) = n(A)/n(S)

Substitute the following values.

P(A) = 1/52

since it can't be reduced farther, the answer is 1/52.

What we know: 52 total cards4 suits (heart,club,diamond, and spade)Each suit has 13 cardsEach suit has an ACE, a KING, a JACK, & a QUEENHearts & Diamonds are REDSpades & Clubs are BLACK

If a card is drawn, find the probability that it is:

A. a spade

There are 13 spades out of 52 cards

Hence, n(S)= 13/52 or 1/4

B. a red card

There are 13 Hearts & 13 Diamonds which are RED, out of 52 cards

(13 + 13 = 26)

Hence, n(S)= 26/52 or 1/2

C. a queen

Each suit has a queen whether it is a diamond, spade, heart or club. So there are a total of 4 queens out of 52 cards.

Hence, n(S)= 4/52 or 1/13

D. a black face card

There are 13 Spades & 13 Clubs which are BLACK, out of 52 cards. (13 +13=26)

Hence, n(S)= 26/52 or 1/2

E. a king or a queen

Just what I have said before, there are a total 4 queens so that means the same goes to the king whether it's a spade, heart, diamond, or club, there are a total of 4 kings.
