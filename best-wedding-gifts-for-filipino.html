5. similarities between korean and filipino wedding​


1. Removal of shoes: Korea, as well as Philippines, practice the removal of their shoes or footwear when entering their homes.
2. Courting and Marriage: Both Koreans and Filipinos give the utmost importance and respect in marriage. As much as possible, they avoid getting in a pre marital situation.
3. Family oriented: Both Philipines and Korea are family oriented. There's even proper roles given to the men and women of the household
4. Traditional Costumes: Korea has hanbok, whereas Philippines have barong ang saya. These are both used on special occasions
5. Courtesy or being courteous: Koreans show their respect by bowing and using proper pronouns in addressing the older generations. They observe hierarchy among themselves. Same as Koreans, Filipinos also use words such as po and opo to show their respect to other people
6. Food: Koreans like mild using mild spices in their dishes, whereas Filipinos use strong ones. Nevertheless, the use of spices is observed in both communities.

25. what is the wedding traditions of filipino​


Answer:

Traditionally the groom’s family pays for the wedding and the grandparents act as the primary witnesses or sponsors. The bride’s gown is often custom made and both the bride and groom wear white. It is bad luck for the bride to try on her dress before the wedding day and to wear pearl jewelry, which is considered a bad omen. The groom wears a sheer, long-sleeve button-up shirt (barong tagalog) that is worn un-tucked over black pants with a white t-shirt underneath.0

The groom and the bride's outfit should be attached with paper bills because it is believed to be good luck.
