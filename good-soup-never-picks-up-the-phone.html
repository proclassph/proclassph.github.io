7. What are the indications of a good quality sauce and soup?​


Answer:

That's a very broad question and one that requires an opinion to answer properly. However, it can also be determined by the application. You should consider the following factors: What is the sauce for? (meat, gravy, marinade, etc.) What is the sauce paired with? (vegetable, dessert, etc.) What will the sauce enhance? (appearance, flavor, texture, acidity, etc.) You need several factors to consider it a good sauce. Consistency (texture), color (grey is NOT a good color for a sauce), and flavor are the three most important but you can't leave out the variables like sweetness, complexity, spice, saltiness, heat, and so on. Otherwise, you'd be pairing a sweet and sour sauce with chocolate cake, and that would be WRONG.
