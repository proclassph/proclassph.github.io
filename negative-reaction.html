7. A spontaneous reaction with a negative change in entropy is exothermic. True False


Answer:

False

Explanation:

A spontaneous reaction with a negative change in entropy is not necessarily always exothermic. The spontaneity of a reaction depends on both the change in entropy and enthalpy of the system. A reaction with negative change in entropy would be spontaneous only if the change in enthalpy is negative (i.e., exothermic). However, it is possible for a reaction with a negative change in entropy to be endothermic (i.e., absorbing heat).

19. Why value of enthalpy of exothermic reactions is negative?​


Answer:

Exothermic Reactions

In an exothermic reaction, energy is released because the total energy of the products is less than the total energy of the reactants. For this reason, the change in enthalpy, ΔH , for an exothermic reaction will always be negative.
