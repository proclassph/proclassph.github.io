The coastal region is a complex area of the Earth's surface, where both aquatic and atmospheric forces create rocky shores, as well as beaches and dunes, walls and tidal inlets and deltas. Atmospheric processes include temperature, moisture and currents, while waves and tides, along with sea temperature and salinity, are the main aquatic processes. The coast also protects diverse habitats, including salt marshes, mangroves, seagrasses and coral reefs. Answer:

These are corrasion, abrasion, hydraulic action, attrition and corrosion/solution. Corrasion is when waves pick up beach material (e.g. pebbles) and hurl them at the base of a cliff. Abrasion occurs as breaking waves which contain sand and larger fragments erode the shoreline or headland.

13. The first philosophers were called ___ because the birthplace of philosophy was in this coastal city, located across the Aegean Sea on the western shores of Ionia in Asia Minor.


Answer:

Milesian philosophers

Explanation:

The first philosophers, or the pre-socratic philosophers, were from the ancient Greek city of Miletus. The philosophy movement o this city started with Thales and his successors,  Anaximander and Anaximenes. Thales' assumption that everything is made of water. His reasoning of what he observed around him made him a philosopher.

Thales' successors expanded and refuted some of his claims. Anaximander agreed that everything is made up of something, but he put forward that matter is made up of something that is not water but instead of what he calls as apeiron. Thales' other successor, Anaximenes, argued that everything is not made of water but of air.

Regardless, the pursuit of knowledge in their desire to explain the composition of the everyday matter around them made these Miletians the first philosophers.
