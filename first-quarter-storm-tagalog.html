1. Ano ang First Quarter storm?


Answer:

The First Quarter Storm (Filipino: Sigwa ng Unang Sangkapat), often shortened into the acronym FQS, was a period of civil unrest in the Philippines which took place during the "first quarter of the year 1970." It included a series of demonstrations, protests, and marches against the administration of President Ferdinand Marcos, mostly organized by students, from January 26 to March 17, 1970.[1][2][3]

Violent dispersals of various FQS protests were among the first watershed events in which large numbers of Filipino students of the 1970s were radicalized against the Marcos administration. Due to these dispersals, many students who had previously held "moderate" positions (i.e., calling for legislative reforms) became convinced that they had no choice but to call for more radical social change.[4]

Similar watershed events would later include the February 1971 Diliman Commune; the August 1971 suspension of the writ of habeas corpus in the wake of the Plaza Miranda bombing; the September 1972 declaration of Martial Law; the 1980 murder of Macli-ing Dulag;[5] the August 1983 assassination of Ninoy Aquino; and eventually, allegations of cheating during the 1986 Snap Elections which led to the non-violent 1986 EDSA Revolution.

5. explain why lightning comes first before thunder diring storms


Though occurring throughout the year, thunderstorms are more likely during the spring and summer months. They originate from the process of convection, where warm air rises, and cold air sinks.

On a warm, moist day, heated air rises and condenses to form cloud, which builds up and eventually precipitates rain. Moving air in the cloud can give off electric charges that lead to the triggering of lightning bolts. While lightning itself is already a potential hazard, the associated hail, winds and heavy downpours of rain in thunderstorms can cause disruption to our infrastructure and transport systems.

The difference between thunder and lightning

Thunder and lightning all occur due to the release of energy – thunder is a soundwave, and lightning is the emission of electromagnetic energy. The reason we see a flash of lightning before hearing thunder is because light travels faster than sound. The speed of light depends on what it is moving through - being slow in gases, fast in liquids, and even quicker in solids. In the air, sound travels at around 332 metres per second. This is a big contrast to the speed of light, that travels about 300 000 kilometres per second.

Compared to light, sound takes its time to travel during a thunderstorm. Scientists have attempted to measure the speed of sound for centuries after Sir Isaac Newton (1643-1727) devised the first theoretical calculation of the speed of sound.

Estimating the distance to lightning

You can figure out how far away lightning is by counting the number of seconds that passes between a flash of lightning and the sound of thunder afterwards. By dividing that number by 5, the result would give you an estimate on how many miles you are from the lightning. So, if we were to count 30 seconds, the lightning would be around 6 miles away.

If you hear thunder, you are already within the area where next ground flashes could happen, as lightning can strike as far away as 10 miles from the centre of a thunderstorm, according to the Met Office. So, if the time delay is very short, and the distance to the lightning is 6 miles or less, it is recommended to seek immediate shelter.

Since light travels faster than sound, the flash of lightning we see before hearing thunder can act as a forewarning, and even tell us how far away the lightning is. If you see a flash of lightning this summer, count the delay till you hear the thunder. If the count is really short, then consider finding shelter!

8. Ibig sabihin ng Storm surge tagalog


A storm surge, storm flood or storm tide is a coastal flood or tsunami-like phenomenon of rising water commonly associated with low pressure weather systems (such as tropical cyclones and strong extratropical cyclones), the severity of which is affected by the shallowness and orientation of the water body relative to storm path, as well as the timing of tides. Most casualties during tropical cyclones occur as the result of storm surges. It is a measure of the rise of water beyond what would be expected by the normal movement related to tides.

The two main meteorological factors contributing to a storm surge are a long fetch of winds spiraling inward toward the storm, and a low-pressure-induced dome of water drawn up under and trailing the storm's center.Sorry my answe is english.

Explanation:

Infrastructure Man - Tumutukoy sa isang tao na responsable sa pagpaplano, pagpapaunlad, at pamamahala ng mga proyekto sa imprastruktura tulad ng mga kalsada, tulay, airport, at iba pang pampublikong gawa.

Writ of habeas corpus - Isang legal na utos na nangangailangan ng isang tao na inaresto o nakapiit na dalhin sa harap ng hukuman o huwes upang masuri ang legalidad ng kanilang pagkakapiit.

Aktibismo - Isang terminong ginagamit upang ilarawan ang aktibong pakikilahok sa mga kilusang panlipunan o pampulitika na naglalayong magpromote ng pagbabago o reporma sa lipunan.

Marcos Cronies - Tumutukoy sa mga taong malapit sa dating Pangulong Ferdinand Marcos na nakinabang sa korapsyon at patronage system ng kanyang rehimen.

First Quarter Storm - Isang panahon ng matinding pag-aalsa ng mga mag-aaral at sibil sa Pilipinas noong unang quarter ng 1970, na naglalayong wakasan ang diktadura ni Ferdinand Marcos.

New People's Army - Isang komunistang grupong gerilya na nagsasagawa ng mga operasyong militar sa Pilipinas na may layuning patalsikin ang gobyerno at magtatag ng isang sosyalistang estado.

MNLF - Ang Moro National Liberation Front ay isang pampulitikang organisasyon na nakabase sa timog na bahagi ng Pilipinas na nagnanais ng mas malawak na awtonomiya o kalayaan para sa mga Muslim-dominated regions sa Mindanao.
