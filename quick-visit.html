3. what is the rhythm of standard waltz?a. slow, quick, slowb. slow, slow, quickc. quick, slow, quickd. quick, quick, slow​


Answer:

Waltz music is written in 3/4 time, counted as "1,2,3 - 1,2,3." The first beat of each measure is accented, corresponding to the extended, highly-stretched step that is taken on the first count, followed by two short steps. With its distinctive rhythm pattern, the Waltz is easy to recognize and simple to learn.

26. quick breads are called "quick" because they use_____as a leavening agent.


Quick breads are called "quick" because they use baking powder or baking soda as a leavening agent. From the word itself 'quick', meaning it is easy and fast to prepare. Examples of quick breads are muffins, cornbreads, and biscuits.

Ingredients for Quick Breads:1 tablespoon sugar2 cups flour1 ½ teaspoon baking powder½ teaspoon baking soda½ teaspoon salt¼ cup melted butter1 cup egg1 cup buttermilk

These ingredients can be mostly found at your homes. Another reason why quick breads are easy and fast to prepare. If some of the ingredients aren't available, it can be bought at the nearest stores at a cheap price making you spend less on baking quick breads.

Summary:

Why are Quick Breads called 'quick'?It uses baking powder or baking soda as a leavening agent, making the baking process easier and quicker.Ingredients of quick breads can be mostly found at your home so it is quickly accessible.If not, it is available at the nearest store.
