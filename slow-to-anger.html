PSALM 145:8

"The Lord is gracious and compassionate, slow to anger, and rich in love" is all about the Lord's love towards us, humans. We all know that we, humans, are sinners from the beginning wherein the Lord sacrificed His only begotten Son to redeem us all from that sin. And yet, here we are making mistakes and sins, again, but the Lord is so gracious and compassionate that we are all given every day the chance to make new beginnings, that we could pray to Him and read His teachings through the Holy Bible so we could talk and have a conversation and be answered. It is also said that the Lord is "slow to anger", indeed because if He's easily angered, where could we be hiding now? Humans are so thankful that the Lord is slow to anger, that we could pray, accept, acknowledge, and, atone for our sins so we could better ourselves, also "rich in love", a very powerful statement it is. The love of the Lord is immeasurable that even we feel like we do not deserve it, we are still accepted and loved. That no matter how big of a failure we think we are, the Lord treats us just like anybody else, or much better, accepts us as His own children. He forgives us all and gives us every day a new chance to better things and correct things. He waits for us to surrender all our worries and walk with Him, because He does not want us to be on the same judgment, once again. Psalm 145:8 surely explains how wide is His arms to hold us all when everything seems to fail.
