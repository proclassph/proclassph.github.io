1. How portable radio did produce the sounds


How radio waves travel from a transmitter to a receiver. Electrons rush up and down the transmitter, shooting out radio waves. The radio waves travel through the air at the speed of light. When the radio waves hit a receiver, they make electrons vibrate inside it, recreating the original signal. This process can happen between one powerful transmitter and many receivers which is why thousands or millions of people can pick up the same radio signal at the same time.

29. Research the following communication devices A. Telephone B. Public Address system C. Portable Radios D. Fire detection and alarm system


A. Telephone: A telephone is a telecommunications device that allows two or more people to communicate over a distance. It typically consists of a handset, which contains the microphone and speaker, and a base unit, which contains the circuitry necessary to connect the handset to the telephone network.

B. Public Address System: A public address system (PA system) is an electronic sound amplification and distribution system with a microphone, amplifier, and loudspeakers used to allow a person to speak to a large public group. It is commonly used in public buildings such as schools, churches, and corporate offices for announcements or speeches.

C. Portable Radios: Portable radios are small radio receivers that can be carried around by hand or worn on the body. They are typically powered by batteries and can be used for listening to music, news broadcasts, sports events, or other audio programming while on the go.

D. Fire Detection and Alarm System: A fire detection and alarm system is an integrated network of devices designed to detect fires in buildings and alert occupants so they can evacuate safely. The system typically consists of smoke detectors, heat detectors, manual call points (pull stations), audible alarms, visual alarms (strobes), control panels, and other components that work together to detect fires quickly and alert occupants in time for them to evacuate safely.
