9. How can you tell that the dark wet spot on the paper towel is fat and not water?


Paper towels are porous materials that can absorb liquid right away. This is why they are very handy at kitchens where spills frequently occur.

However, absorbency can also be affected by the viscosity of the liquid. A liquid with higher viscosity, meaning thick or slimy, can be absorbed less by the paper towel.

One of the most distinguishable difference between water and oil is the viscosity. Oil has the higher viscosity because it is thicker. So even if you do not check the paper towel, you know that a sheet of paper towel will absorb less oil. That is why when you need to clean spilled oil from the counter top, you would need more paper towels, or sometimes you may even need to use a rag and soap.

Water on the other hand is easily absorbed because it a very light liquid. It permeates the surface of the paper towel easily.

Now going back on your question, how can you tell if the dark spot is oil or liquid? There are several ways. Let's say, hypothetically for this experiment, that there is an equal amount of oil and water, and there is the same amount and quality of paper towel used to absorb the two spills.

If the paper towel has a bigger wet spot, it means it absorbed more liquid. That would be the towel used for water. If the spot is smaller, that is the one used for oil.If the paper towel is taken of the counter top or table where the spill was, check for any remaining spills. If the paper towel has absorbed all the liquid, the spill was from water. If there is a remaining film of liquid, that is from the oil.
