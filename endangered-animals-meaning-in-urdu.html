26. when is an animal endangered?​


Answer:

An animal is considered endangered when it is at risk of extinction. This can occur for a variety of reasons, including habitat loss, overhunting, climate change, and disease. Endangered animals may be protected by national and international conservation efforts, but they are still in danger of disappearing from the earth. It is important to take action to help protect and preserve endangered species.

Answer:

An animal is considered endangered when there are so few individuals left that the species is at risk of becoming extinct. This can be due to a variety of factors, such as habitat destruction, overhunting, pollution, disease, or a combination of these and other threats. In order to be classified as endangered, a species must meet certain criteria set by national and international organizations, such as the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN). The IUCN uses a classification system to evaluate the conservation status of species and determine whether they are at risk of extinction. Animals that are classified as endangered are considered to be facing a very high risk of extinction in the wild.


27. what are the extinct animals and endangered animals?


Answer:

Extinct Animals and Endangered Animals

Extinct Animals

Extinction of a particular animal occurs when there are no more individuals of that species alive anywhere in the world. The species has died out. Extinct animals are the animals that do not exist anymore.

Examples of Extinct Animals:

Dinosaur (extinct for 65 million years)

Passenger pigeon (extinct for 100 years)

Saber-toothed tiger (extinct for about 10,000 years)

Dodo (extinct for 332 years)

Great auk (extinct for 170 years)

Pyrean ibex (extinct for 13 years)

Giant ground sloth (extinct for 10,000 years)

Aurochs (extinct for 400 years)

Tasmanian tiger (extinct for About 70 years)

Giant rodent (extinct for 2-4 million years)

Haast’s eagle (extinct for 500 years)

Endangered Animals

Endangered animals are any species that is at risk of extinction because of a sudden rapid decrease in its population or a loss of its critical habitat. There are so few of them that they might soon be wiped out altogether.

Examples of Endangered Animals:

Amur leopard (less than 70 individuals exist today)

Sea turtles (Two types of sea turtles are amongst the most endangered species in the world: the Hawksbill Turtle and the Leatherback Turtle.)

Orangutan (approximately 80% of the population lost)

Sumatran elephant (less than 2000 are estimated to exist)

Vaquita (less than 100 thought to exist)
