15. Your own opinion about the theory of Prof. Pankaj Ghemawat in "We live in a multidomestic world, not a flat one."​


Answer:

Prof. Pankaj Ghemawat's theory challenges the popular notion that we live in a flat world, where technology and globalization have erased the boundaries between nations and cultures. Instead, Ghemawat argues that we live in a multidomestic world, where local differences and idiosyncrasies still play a significant role in shaping economic and social interactions.

Ghemawat's theory suggests that businesses and organizations must adapt their strategies to account for the cultural, political, and economic differences across different regions and countries. Rather than assuming that one size fits all, companies must tailor their products and services to meet the unique needs and preferences of local markets.

The theory has important implications for policymakers, businesses, and individuals. It underscores the importance of cultural competence and cross-cultural communication in a globalized world. It also challenges the notion that technology and globalization will inevitably lead to a homogenized world and emphasizes the importance of diversity and cultural exchange.

Overall, Ghemawat's theory provides a valuable perspective on the complexities of our globalized world and highlights the importance of recognizing and embracing cultural differences.
