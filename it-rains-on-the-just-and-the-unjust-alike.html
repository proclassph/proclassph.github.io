2. what is just at unjust law?​


Answer:

A just law is a man made code that squares with the moral law or the law of God. An unjust law is a code that is out of harmony with the moral law. To put it in the terms of St. Thomas Aquinas: An unjust law is a human law that is not rooted in eternal law and natural law.

6. Write just if the action of a character is reasonable and unjust if it is not. Bernard did not brag of his achievement to Willy.


Answer:

Just

Explanation:

It is never okay to brag about the achievements one has obtained in life.  Bragging will make another feel inferior of himself, while the one bragging only exhibits superiority over the other.

Sharing about one’s achievements is good, but to brag about them is inappropriate.

Achievements are meant to be someone’s reflection of their success, and a manifestation of their hard work and sacrifices. It should not make others feel incapable of achieving the same level of success, simply because every individual has a personal view of the measure of their success.

Therefore, it is just for Bernard to not brag about his achievements to Willy.

11. Write just if the action of a character is reasonable and unjust if it is not. Happy admitted that it is all right to lie for someone’s convenience.


Answer:

Just

Explanation:

We were taught that lying is as bad as a crime.  However, some circumstances may demand us to lie.

Lies are acceptable if the motive is not selfish. For example, if someone asked you if they are beautiful, you could say, Yes, because they simply are and you want them to feel better and happy about themselves.  But if you answered, Yes, just to finish the conversation, then that is when the wrong motive enters.

Lying can also be diverted to become a constructive criticism instead.  For example, if an employee exhibits a low performance at work, it would be better to say this, “You are just starting but you can surely do better”.  It does not sound demeaning, rather it could even boost the employee’s motivation.
