Answer:

LAMB WAVES.

or Rolling Waves

Explanation:

Rayleigh waves are a type of surface acoustic wave that travel along the surface of solids. ... When guided in layers they are referred to as Lamb waves, Rayleigh–Lamb waves, or generalized Rayleigh waves.

Rayleigh waves are sometimes called rolling waves. In Rayleigh waves the surface of the earth rises up and sinks down in crests and troughs, similar to waves on the surface of water.


10. characteristic of rayleigh wave


Rayleigh waves are surface waves first found by Rayleigh (1885). Particle motion of Rayleigh waves in a half-space is elliptical and retrograde at the surface. The amplitude attenuates with depth. Rayleigh waves are dispersive in a stratified half-space.


11. Jhomel is 5 times as old as Kayleigh. In 16 years, Jhomel's age will be a year less than twice Kayleigh's age. If I was three times as old as Kayleigh 4 years hence. How old am I?​


Answer:

23 years old

Step-by-step explanation:

This is a two-step age problem. First, we need to find the age of Kayleigh. Then, we need to find the age of the speaker using the information.

Let's start making the equation for Kayleigh's age. Let k be her current age. Accordingly, Jhomel's current age will be 5k.

Now that we have declared our variables, we can now turn the second sentence of the question into symbols.

5k + 16 = 2(k+16) - 1

Now, we can start solving this...

5k + 16 = 2k + 32 - 1

5k - 2k = 32 - 17

3k = 15

k = 5

Thus, Kayleigh's current age is 5.

Now, we need to find the age of the speaker "I." We will represent his or her current age, the final answer, x.

From the third sentence of the question, we can again start making another equation.

x + 4 = 3 (k + 4)

Now, remember that k is Kayleigh's age and we already have discovered it; it's 5. Using that, we need to substitute 5 into our k in the equation.

x + 4 = 3 [(5) + 4]

Solving this,

x + 4 = 3 (9)

x + 4 = 27

x = 27 - 4

x = 23

Therefore, the age of the speaker is 23 years old.

14. what is Rayleigh wave​


Rayleigh waves are a type of surface acoustic wave that travel along the surface of solids. They can be produced in materials in many ways, such as by a localized impact or by piezo-electric transduction, and are frequently used in non-destructive testing for detecting defects

Rayleigh waves are a type of surface wave that travel near the surface of solids. Rayleigh waves include both longitudinal and transverse motions that decrease exponentially in amplitude as distance from the surface increases. There is a phase difference between these component motions.

Explanation:

Love and Rayleigh waves are guided by the free surface of the Earth. They follow along after the P and S waves have passed through the body of the planet. Both Love and Rayleigh waves involve horizontal particle motion, but only the latter type has vertical ground.

Two types of surface waves, known as Love waves and Rayleigh waves after their discoverers, propagate near the Earth's surface. Rayleigh waves result from a combination of P and SV motions, while Love waves result from SH waves trapped near the surface. Due to their slower rate of decay, surface waves can circle.
