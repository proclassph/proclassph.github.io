1. What is a motherboard? Explain why the motherboard is important in a computer?​


Answer:

A printed circuit board containing the principal components of a computer or other device, with connectors into which other circuit boards can be slotted.

Explanation:

The motherboard provides a socket into which a CPU can be fitted along with slots for the RAM, internal ports for storage devices and external ports for peripherals. Expansion ports such as PCI/PCI-e are also provided which allow expansion cards to be fitted for additional functionality.

8. what is the meaning of motherboard?​


What is the meaning of motherboard?​

A motherboard is the main circuit board of a computer that connects all of the components together. It includes CPU, RAM, and expansion card sockets, as well as connections and wires for hard drives, disc drives, and front panel interfaces of a computer.

A computer motherboard is also known as its mainboard, logic board, system board, mobos, and MBs. It links the many components of a computer while also allowing the CPU to access and control them. In addition to bridging internal components, the motherboard ports may be utilized to connect external devices to the computer. External devices include monitors, headphones, speakers, microphones, keyboards, mice, modems, and other USB devices.
