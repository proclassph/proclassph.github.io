10. Differentiate estuary from interdinal zone


ESTUARY vs. INTERTIDAL ZONEWhat's the difference?

ESTUARY

body of water that is actually found near the coastthis is where fresh waters (in rivers) and streams runsthis is where the tides are actually rises and falls the salinity (saltiness of a water) and water level

Different kinds of estuary:

Bar-built estuary

an estuary that is formed when the ocean currents and the eaves in coastal areas is being bordered by sandbars and/or barrier lands, in order to shield up the bay or lagoon

Tectonic Estuaries

an estuary that is caused by rift on Earth's crust

Coastal Plain Estuaries

an estuary that is made if the sea level in river valleys will ascend or descend

Delta System Estuary

an estuary formed by the prohibition of a delta to river to flow

Fjords Estuaries

an U-shaped estuary because of the actions within the glacier

INTERTIDAL ZONE

a place where the sea encounters the land

Zones within the Intertidal Zone

High Tide Zone

a place where the high tide overflows

Middle Tide Zone

a place where salt water overflows and not overflowed

Low Tide Zone

a place that is submerged of water

12. Which statement is true regarding interim financial statements?


Answer:

Interim financial statement:

Interim financial statements are financial statements that cover a period of less than one year. They are used to convey information about the performance of the issuing entity prior to the end of the normal reporting year, and so are closely followed by investors.

Interim financial statement in accounting:

a financial reporting period shorter than a full financial year (most typically a quarter or half-year)

Components of an interim financial statement:

interim financial report should include, at a minimum, the following components: (a) condensed balance sheet; (b) condensed statement of profit and loss; (c) condensed cash flow statement; and (d) selected explanatory notes.

29. facts about crabs in mangroves tree in interdidal zone in estuaries​


Mangrove trees can be found in intertidal zones in estuaries, which are areas where freshwater from rivers mixes with saltwater from the ocean. These trees play a vital role in protecting coastlines from erosion and providing habitat for a variety of species, including crabs. Here are some facts about crabs that live in mangrove trees in intertidal zones in estuaries:

Mangrove crabs, also known as rhizophora crabs, are common in mangrove forests and play an important role in the ecosystem by breaking down leaf litter and recycling nutrients back into the soil.These crabs are adapted to living in the intertidal zone, where they can tolerate fluctuations in salinity and oxygen levels.Mangrove crabs are omnivorous and feed on a variety of plant and animal matter, including leaves, seeds, insects, and small fish.Some species of mangrove crabs are economically important as a source of food for local communities.Mangrove crabs are threatened by habitat destruction, pollution, and overharvesting. It is important to protect these ecosystems and the species that depend on them.

Answer:

1.Crabs are common inhabitants of mangrove forests, particularly in the intertidal zone of estuaries.

2.Mangrove crabs play an important role in the ecosystem by scavenging for organic matter and helping to recycle nutrients.

3.Some species of mangrove crabs are adapted to living in the muddy and oxygen-poor conditions found in the intertidal zone by being able to extract oxygen from the air through specialized structures called branchiostegal lungs.

4.The distribution and abundance of mangrove crabs can be influenced by various factors such as tidal fluctuations, temperature, and salinity.

5.Many mangrove crabs are important food sources for larger animals, such as birds and fish, and they also have cultural and economic value in some areas where they are harvested for food by humans
