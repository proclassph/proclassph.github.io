3. what do remember about Pre Spanish Lterature​


Answer:

The Pre-Spanish Period

Long before the Spaniards and other foreigners landed or set foot on Philippine shores, our forefathers already had their own literature stamped in the history of our race.

Our ancient literature shows our customs and traditions in everyday life as traced in our folk stories, old plays and short stories. Our ancestors also had their own alphabet which was different from that brought by the Spaniards. The first alphabet used by our ancestor was similar to that of the Malayo-Polynesian alphabet. Whatever records our ancestors left were either burned by the Spaniards friars in the belief that they were works of the devil or were written on materials that easily perished, like the barks of trees, dried leaves and bamboo cylinders which could not have remained undestroyed even if efforts were made to preserve them. Other records that remained showed folk songs that proved the existence of a native culture truly our own. Some of these were passed on byword of mouth till they reached the hands of some publishers or printers who took interest in printing the manuscripts of the ancient Filipinos. The Spaniards who came to the Philippines tried to prove that our ancestors were really fond of poetry, songs, stories, riddles and proverbs which we still enjoy today and which serve to show to generations the true culture of our people.


4. At the end of the 21st century, this pandemic will still be remembered becuase of its big effect to humanity like the Spanish Influenza 1918


The Spanish Influenza of 1918 is one of the deadliest pandemics in history. It killed more than 50 million people around the world, and it changed the way people think about disease and public health. The effects of the pandemic are still felt today.

In the same way, the effects of the 21st century pandemic will be remembered for many years to come. It will be marked as a time when society had to completely change its way of living, and when people had to confront the reality of a virus that had the potential to affect the entire world. It will also be remembered as a time when humanity came together in the face of an unknown and unprecedented danger, and when science, technology, and medicine helped to contain and eventually defeat the virus. We will also remember the great loss of life and the economic, social, and political impacts that resulted from the pandemic.
