4. Two bells ring at different intervals. Bell A rings every 20 minutes. Bell B rings every 30 minutes. If both bells ring together at 8:30 a.m., at what time will the two bells ring together again?


Answer:

5:00 pm

Step-by-step explanation:

So first you find the LCM, or least common multiple, (can be called LCD, least common denominator).

The LCM of 20, 30, and 50 is basically 10⋅2⋅3⋅5 because you factor out the 10 since that is a common factor.

10⋅2⋅3⋅5=300

This is the number of minutes. To find the number of hours, you simply divide by 60 and get 5 hours. Then you count 5 more hours from 12:00 pm and get 5:00 pm

7. Two bells ring at an interval of 6and 8 minutes respectevely if they ring at 9:15am at what time they will ring together?


Answer:

9:39 am

Step-by-step explanation:

In simple way, we need to find the lowest number that is fully divisible by 6 and 8. So let's find the LCM of these two, and that is 24.

So 24 minutes after 9:15 am, both bells will ring together again, which is 9:39 am.

    The Ring of Fire or what we called as the Circum-Pacific Belt is a way along the Pacific Ocean portrayed by active volcanoes and successive seismic tremors. Most of the Earth's volcanoes and quakes happens and occur along and on the Ring of Fire.

Why is the ring of fire dotted with volcano?

    The abundance of volcanoes and seismic tremors along the Ring of Fire is brought about by the measure of development of structural plates in the zone. Along a significant part of the Ring of Fire, plates cover at joined limits called subduction zones. The plate that is underneath is pushed down, or subducted by the plate above. More than 30 active volcanoes and many lethargic and wiped out volcanoes are in two significant volcanic belts.
