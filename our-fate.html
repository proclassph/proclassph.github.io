Answer:

8x + 3x + 11 + 4 = 180

11x + 15 = 180

11x = 180 - 15

11x = 165 [divide both sides by 11 to cancel x ]

x = 15

Angle E is equal to Angle A since they are opposite angles.

Angle E = 8x + 11 [ we got 15 from the value above ]

= 8(15) + 11

= 120 + 11

= 131°

Therefore, Angle A is equal to 131°

3.

Angle T = 3x + 4

= 3(15) + 4

= 49°

13. WHAT IS THE FATE OF OUR SUN AFTER ANOTHER 5 BILLION YEARS?


Answer:

But in about 5 billion years, the sun will run out of hydrogen. Our star is currently in the most stable phase of its life cycle and has been since the birth of our solar system, about 4.5 billion years ago. Once all the hydrogen gets used up, the sun will grow out of this stable phase. With no hydrogen left to fuse in the core, a shell of fusion hydrogen will form around the helium-filled core, astrophysicist Jillian Scudder wrote in an article for The Conversation. Gravitational forces will take over, compressing the core and allowing the rest of the sun to expand. Our star will grow to be larger than we can imagine — so large that it'll envelope the inner planets, including Earth. That's when the sun will become a red giant.

17. so often we accept the declarations that others have made concerning our own lives, well being or fate​


Answer:

As human beings, we have a tendency to internalize the opinions and beliefs of others, especially those who hold positions of authority or influence in our lives. This can lead us to accept the declarations that others have made concerning our own lives, well-being, or fate without question.

We may be told by a doctor that we have a certain medical condition and we may accept it as the absolute truth without seeking a second opinion or doing our own research. We may be told by a teacher that we are not good at a particular subject and we may believe that we are not capable of improving in that area. We may be told by a parent or friend that we are not attractive or intelligent enough and we may believe that this is true.

However, it is important to remember that our lives, well-being, and fate are ultimately our own responsibility. We have the power to question the declarations made by others and to make our own decisions based on our own beliefs and values.

This does not mean that we should disregard the opinions and advice of others, especially those who have our best interests at heart. It simply means that we should take a critical and thoughtful approach to the information that is presented to us, and make our own informed decisions based on our own research and intuition.

By taking ownership of our lives and our well-being, we can empower ourselves to make positive changes and to live the life that we truly want, rather than the life that others have declared for us. We have the power to shape our own fate, and it is up to us to exercise that power wisely and responsibly.
