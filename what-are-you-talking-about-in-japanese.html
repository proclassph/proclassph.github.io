Romulo'sworks?a) They talk about the love of Filipinos toward theiroppressorsb) They talk about how the Philippines survived the warsduring the American and the Japanese occupations.They talk about how the Filipinos are grateful for beingcolonized by the Americans and the Japanese.d) They talk about how Filipinos look up to Spaniardsand Americans.​


Answer:

B.

Explanation:

Carlos Peña Romulo, QSC PLH (14 January 1898 – 15 December 1985) was a Filipino diplomat, statesman, soldier, journalist and author. He was a reporter at 16, a newspaper editor by the age of 20, and a publisher at 32. He was a co-founder of the Boy Scouts of the Philippines, a general in the US Army and the Philippine Army, university president, President of the UN General Assembly, was eventually named one of the Philippines' National Artists in Literature, and was the recipient of many other honors and honorary degrees. His hometown is Camiling, Tarlac and he studied at the Camiling Central Elementary School during his basic education.

18. what have you learned about Chinese, Japanese, and Korean Arts?


Answer:

From what I know, Chinese, Japanese, and Korean arts have a long and rich history and are characterized by various artistic traditions and styles. Here are some things that I have learned about the arts of these three East Asian cultures:

- Chinese art: Chinese art is a diverse field that encompasses various mediums, including painting, sculpture, ceramics, and architecture. Chinese art is known for its emphasis on balance, harmony, and symbolism. Some notable styles of Chinese art include Chinese calligraphy, traditional Chinese painting, and opera.

- Japanese art: Japanese art is characterized by its attention to detail, simplicity, and the use of natural materials. Some notable Japanese art styles include ukiyo-e woodblock printing, kabuki theater, and the tea ceremony. Japanese art is also known for its use of natural elements, such as bamboo and stone, and its emphasis on the beauty of imperfection.

- Korean art: Korean art is known for its bright colors, intricate patterns, and emphasis on the natural world. Some notable styles of Korean art include Korean calligraphy, traditional Korean painting, and Korean pottery. Korean art is also characterized by its use of natural materials, such as clay, stone, and wood, and its focus on the beauty of the imperfect.
