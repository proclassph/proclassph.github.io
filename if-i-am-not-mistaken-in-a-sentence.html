29. A. You are mistaken B. You might be mistakenIs the meaning of the sentences the same or different?explain why​


Explanation:

A is straight forward, you are mistaken, it is as simple as that, your are mistaken. B on the other hand implies a unsure certainty on the speaker's part, since the speaker does not think the person is "mistaken" straightly, but rather implies the person "might" be mistaken. Thus, the speaker does not know, or tries to use other language to imply a not so straightforward answer, but rather "might". The speaker is unsure if the person is mistaken or not. To sum it up, A is straightforward speaking that the person is mistaken, while B is speaking that the person "might" be mistaken but possibly not also because of an uncertainty in the speakers knowledge if indeed the person is mistaken or just "might be".
