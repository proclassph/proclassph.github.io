20. Using a map plotted against a Cartesian plane, the location of a hospital is at (- 8, - 5) while the pharmacy nearest it is at (- 2, 3) How far is the pharmacy from the hospital if the map is on a scale of 1 unit= 0. 5kilomete ?.


Answer:

To find the distance between two points on a Cartesian plane, we can use the Pythagorean theorem, which states that the distance between two points (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) is given by the square root of the sum of the squares of the differences of their x-coordinates and y-coordinates:

distance = √((x2 - x1)^2 + (y2 - y1)^2)

In this case, the hospital is located at (-8, -5) and the pharmacy is located at (-2, 3), so the distance between them can be calculated as follows:

distance = √((-2 - (-8))^2 + (3 - (-5))^2)

distance = √(6^2 + 8^2)

distance = √(36 + 64)

distance = √100

distance = 10

Since the map is on a scale of 1 unit = 0.5 kilometers, the distance between the hospital and the pharmacy is 10 units * 0.5 kilometers/unit = 5 kilometers.
