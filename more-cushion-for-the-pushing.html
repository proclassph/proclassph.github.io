11. what are the potential benefits of implementing more inclusive policies to cushion the negative consequences of globalization,and how can these policies narrow the gap between winners and lossers?​


Answer:

1. Increased Economic Growth: Inclusive policies can help to promote economic growth by creating a more diverse and resilient economy that can adapt to changing circumstances. More people participating in economic activities means more businesses, more jobs, and more growth.

2. Improved Social Welfare: Inclusive policies can also contribute to a more equal distribution of wealth and resources, which can improve social welfare. Increased access to education, healthcare, and food security for all members of society can lead to a more stable and prosperous society.

3. Reduced Inequality: Inclusive policies can help to reduce inequality between winners and losers. Through measures such as progressive taxation and social safety nets, more resources can be directed towards those who are struggling most to keep up with the pace of globalization.

4. Better Resilience: Inclusive policies can help to create a more resilient society that is better equipped to manage the risks associated with globalization, such as job displacement and economic shocks. When more people have access to education and training, they are better equipped to adapt to changing circumstances and take advantage of new opportunities.

5. More Political Stability: Inclusive policies can also contribute to greater political stability, by reducing the risk of social unrest and conflict that can arise from disenfranchised groups. When more people are included in the benefits of globalization, they are more likely to be supportive of the system as a whole.

Through these benefits, more inclusive policies can help to narrow the gap between winners and losers, creating a more equitable and just society.

15. a tailors/pressing ham is best described as?a.) firm round cushionb.) smooth flat cushionc.) rough round cushiond.) soft flat cushion​


Answer:

A tailor's ham or dressmaker's ham is a tightly stuffed pillow used as a curved mold when pressing curved areas of clothing, such as darts, sleeves, cuffs, collars, or waistlines. Pressing on a curved form allows a garment better to fit body contours.
