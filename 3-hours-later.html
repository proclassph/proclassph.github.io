4. John watched television for 1 hour 35 minutes. Later he read. He watched television and read for a total of 3 hours 52 minutes. How long did John read?


Answer: John read for 2 hours and 17 minutes.

Explanation:

Can we subtract this to know the answer? In this particular case, yes. Simply subtract the hours first and then the minutes.

 3 hours and 52 minutes- 1 hour   and 35 minutes= 2 hours and 17 minutes

However, this is not applicable to all cases in this type of word problem. To make this simpler, we can convert the hours and minutes to minutes.

1 hour = 60 minutes

TV and Reading time:

3 hours and 52 minutes3 (60) and 52 minutes180 + 52 minutes232 minutes

TV time:

1 hour and 35 minutes1 (60) and 35 minutes60 + 35 minutes95 minutes

Reading time:

232 minutes - 95 minutes137 minutes

If we want to get the number of hours, we must identify the maximum number of hours there is in 137 minutes. If we divide it by 60, the answer will be 2.28333..., so we take the whole number 2 as 2 hours, then subtract the remaining to get the minutes.

2 hours × 60 = 120 minutes(Reading time) 137 minutes - 120 = 17 (excess minutes)

Therefore, John read for a total of 2 hours and 17 minutes.

12. 3. A biologist is researching a newly- discovered species of bacteria. At time t = 0 hours, he puts one hundred bacteria into what he has determined to be a favorable growth medium. Four hours later, he found out that the total bacteria is 400. Give an exponential model for the situation. How many bacteria 12 hours?​


Answer:

An exponential model for the situation can be represented by the equation:

y = 100e^kt

where y is the number of bacteria at time t, 100 is the initial number of bacteria at t = 0, and k is the growth rate constant.

To find the value of k, we can use the information given:

At t = 4 hours, there are 400 bacteria:

400 = 100e^(4k)

Solving for k, we get:

k = ln(4) / 4

Using this value of k, we can find the number of bacteria at t = 12 hours:

y = 100e^(12k)

y = 100e^(12ln(4) / 4)

y = 100 * 4^3

y = 6400 bacteria

So there will be 6400 bacteria after 12 hours

25. John watched television for 1 hour 35 minutes. Later he read. He watched television and read for a total of 3 hours 52 minutes. How long did John read?


Answer:

2 hours and 17 minutes

Step-by-step explanation:

First, we will convert the given time to minutes. To do that, we will multiply 60 to the given hour then add the given minutes to the product.

Watched:

1 hr 35 mins

1 (60) + 35

60 + 35

95 minutes

Watched and Read:

3 hrs + 52 mins

3 (60) + 52

180 + 52

232 minutes

Now, we will subtract the total minutes that he read and watched to the time he just watched.

232 - 95

137 minutes

Finally, we will convert it back to hours. To do that, we will divide 137 by 60. The remainder would be the number of minutes.

[tex]\frac{137}{60}[/tex]

2 hrs 17 mins
