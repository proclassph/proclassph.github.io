2. A special class is made up of 12 boys and 8 girls. Four officers are selected. How many different sets of officers will contain a president and secretary from the girls and vice-president and secretary from the boys?


There are 8 girls and 12 boys in the class, so we can choose the president and secretary from the 8 girls in 8C2 ways (i.e., choosing 2 girls out of 8), and we can choose the vice-president and treasurer from the 12 boys in 12C2 ways (i.e., choosing 2 boys out of 12).

Using the multiplication principle, the number of different sets of officers will be:

8C2 x 12C2 = (8 x 7 / 2 x 1) x (12 x 11 / 2 x 1) = 28 x 66 = 1,848

Therefore, there are 1,848 different sets of officers that contain a president and secretary from the girls and vice-president and secretary from the boys.

5. Which of the following situations demonstrate the use of simple machines? A. A girl eats a sandwichB. A boy runs across a football fieldC. A father drives a car to the officeD. A mother pushes stroller up a ramp​


Of the given options, D is the situation that demonstrates the use of a simple machine.

Detail of explanation:

A simple machine is a tool to help human activities in the simplest form that only needs one movement to use

There are some examples of simple planes

1. Pulley

A pulley is a simple machine in the form of a wheel wrapped around a rope. At the end of the rope, there is a container that can be used to raise or lower objects. Pulley is used by being pulled when raising and stretched when lowering.

2. Wedge

A wedge is a pointed object to separate a substance.  The way to use a wedge is to place the pointed end of the wedge against an object and push the wedge hard.  Wedges are usually used to split wood. The jet's tip design also uses the wedge principle to help split the air and clouds.

3. Inclined plane

An inclined plane is a simple machine for moving heavy items on an inclined surface.  The slope of the object determines how much work is required.  the more inclined the bigger effort required.

4. Screw

A screw is a pointed object with a cylindrical shape. A screw is used as a fastener when attaching metal or wood, it must be installed by turning.

5. Lever

A lever is a long beam that is stacked on an axle. The object to be lifted is placed at one end of the block while the other end is pushed to lift the object.

6. Wheel and axle

The wheel and axle have the concept of a circular wheel connected to the axle.  the wheel will spin at its axle.  Examples of simple planes with the concept of wheels and axles are the wheels on cars, and also on the steering wheel.

Let us discuss the options above

1. A girl eating a sandwich is not a situation that demonstrates the use of a simple machine because to eat a sandwich the girl doesn't use a simple machine.

2. A boy running across a football field is not a situation that demonstrates the use of a simple machine because the boy doesn't use a simple plane

3. A father driving a car to the office is not a situation that demonstrates the use of a simple machine. Although a car has wheels and a steering wheel that uses the simple plane concept of a wheel and axle, the car is a complex machine that requires more than one engine and movement to operate.

4. D choice is a situation demonstrating the use of a simple machine because a pushcart is a simple machine that has wheels and a ramp is an example of a simple plane with the concept of an inclined plane.
