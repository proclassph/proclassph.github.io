Explanation:

Collecting match-boxes is one of his favorite pastimes. (singular subject "Collecting match-boxes" takes a singular verb "is")

The quality of the candies is poor. (singular subject "quality" takes a singular verb "is")

Neither his father nor his brothers play quitter. (plural subject "father and brothers" takes a plural verb "play")

Neither of my parents is fond of watching variety shows. (singular subject "neither" takes a singular verb "is"; "parents" is the correct plural noun form)

None of my friends are there. (plural subject "friends" takes a plural verb "are")
