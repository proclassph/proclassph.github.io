The chart should have a single box at the top representing the head of the family, with boxes branching off beneath it to represent other family members. You can use lines to connect each box to show the relationships between family members and their roles in the family.

When delegating tasks within a family, it is essential to consider everyone's strengths and weaknesses, interests, and availability. You can start by making a list of all the tasks that need to be done, and then assigning them based on each family member's capabilities and availability. It is also important to communicate clearly about expectations and ensure that everyone understands their roles and responsibilities.

During the interviews with your parents, you can ask them about their views on delegating tasks within the family. Some possible questions to ask include:

How do you decide who should do what tasks in the family?

Do you think it's important to delegate tasks fairly in the family? Why or why not?

How do you ensure that everyone in the family contributes to household tasks?

Have there been any challenges or conflicts related to task delegation in the family? How were they resolved?

Based on their responses, you can gain a better understanding of how your family handles task delegation and identify areas for improvement. You can then use this information to update your functional organizational chart and ensure that everyone in the family is involved and contributing to the family's success.
