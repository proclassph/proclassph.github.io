5. There are 12 men on the basketball team. 2 are centers, 5 are guards, the rest a forwards. What is the probability, as a percent, that out of two players chosen at random they would be a guard and a forward?


Solution:

There are 12 men on the basketball team. 2 are centers, 5 are guards, the rest a forwards. What is the probability, as a percent, that out of two players chosen at random they would be a guard and a forward?

So, we have 12 men in a basketball team which means, we have,
n(S) = 12.

Then, the probability that out of two players chosen at random they would be a guard and a forward, we know that it has 5 guards and 5 forwards, so,
n(E) = 5 for guards and,
n(E) = 5 for forwards.

Thus,

5/12 for the probability of guards and 5/12 for the forwards,
By this values, we multiplied it to get the probability that out of two players chosen at random they would be a guard and a forward,

5/12 x 5/12
25/144

Therefore,
17.36% is the probability that out of two players chosen at random they would be a guard and a forward.

23. Problem 1. There are 60 students in the Department of Mathematics at the College of Education. One-forth of them are members of the basketball team, and one-fifth are members of the volleyball team. If three students are members of both basketball and volleyball team and the rest of the students belong in the baseball team, how many are members of the baseball team?


Answer:

33

Step-by-step explanation:

Since one-fourth of the 60 students are members of the basketball team since one-fourth of 60 is 15 (multiply 60 and 1/4). On the other hand, 12 students are members of the volleyball team (multiply 60 and 1/5). Then add 12 and 15. We get 27. To know the number of the members of the baseball team, which is the remaining number of students, we just have to subtract 27 from 60 and we get 33.
