10. The lines that travel from west to east and that are highlighted in yellow on this map are __________


The lines that travel from west to east and that are highlighted in yellow on this map are called the lines of latitude. They are 180 parallel lines  (parallel to the equator line) that indicate or measure north or south positions from the equator. The equator stands at 0 degrees while the North Pole is at 90 degrees north. Conversely, the South Pole is at 90 degrees south.  

One of the most famous locations that is commonly referred to by their latitudinal location is the 38th parallel which approximately demarcates North and South Korea. This was a name given by United States planner to the area, towards the end of the Korean War.  

Meanwhile, the Philippines is located at 12.8797° N, 121.7740° E. This means that we are approximately 13 degrees north of the equator, making our weather hot, compared to those further north.
