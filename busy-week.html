20. alfred lost 2500 in his business in the first week. if he gains 5,000 in the following week, will he have a gain or a loss? and how much gain or loss will he have?​


Answer ﻿and Step by stepe explanation:

If Alfred lost $2500 in the first week of his business and gained $5000 in the following week, we can calculate his overall gain or loss by subtracting the total losses from the total gains.

Total Gains = $5000

Total Losses = $2500

Overall Gain/Loss = Total Gains - Total Losses

= $5000 - $2500

= $2500

Therefore, Alfred will have an overall gain of $2500 after gaining $5000 in the second week, despite the initial loss of $2500 in the first week.

Answer:

Alfred's net gain or loss will depend on his initial investment and other expenses. However, based on the given information, we can calculate his net gain or loss for the two weeks as follows:

Net gain or loss = Total income - Total expenses

In the first week, Alfred lost 2500. This means his total expenses for the week were 2500.

In the second week, he gained 5000. This means his total income for the week was 5000.

Therefore, his net gain or loss for the two weeks can be calculated as:

Net gain or loss = 5000 - 2500 = 2500

Since his net gain is positive, Alfred will have a gain of 2500.
