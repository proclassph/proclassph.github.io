1. Find the measure of angel AEB


Finding the Angle's Measure

A triangle's angles are always added together to equal 180 degrees. So, to find the unknown angles in a triangle, subtract the supplied angles from 180.

Problem:

Find the mAEC and mAEB from the measures of the angles (m) DEC = 56° and DEB = 124°. Find the angle's AEB measurement.

Solution:

A. Since angle DEA is made up of the two supplementary angles DEB and AEB and angle DEB has a length of 124 degrees, we have:

[tex]180 = 124 + AEB\\AEB = 180 – 124\\AEB = 56[/tex]

AEB thus has a 56 degree measure.

B. Similar to this, because angles DEC and AEC are supplementary and angle DEC measures 56 degrees, we obtain

[tex]180 = AEC + 56\\AEC = 180 – 56\\AEC = 124[/tex]

As a result, the angle AEC has a 124 degree measure.
