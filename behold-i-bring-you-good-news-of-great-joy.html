16. Weekly Question: Would you rather hear The hurtful truth that brings you guilt towards the end or The beautiful lie that brings you painful joy to your friends?


Answer:

I believe the painful truth is far better than beautiful lies.

The truth only hurts when it injures one’s ego. Sometimes people aren’t humble enough to gracefully admit they are wrong; other times people aren’t comfortable accepting that someone they love or admire is not the person they believed them to be. Whatever the cause of the pain is, it is - at least, in part - due to a person’s inability to cope with the truth. The pain of the truth can be overcome with the proper attitude, patience, and practice.

Beautiful lies are far worse. If someone accept propositions because their appeal instead of their veracity, it is easy for them to manipulate by others or simply become a victim of fate. As long as they remain ignorant or refuse to acknowledge the truth, they will continue to act against their own self-interests and/or the common good. The refusal to pursue the truth also encourages complacency and discourages skepticism; no one will expand their knowledge or otherwise grow if they are unwilling to challenge that which is presented to them.
