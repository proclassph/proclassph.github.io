During the segmental phase of the pharyngeal arch development, pharyngeal pouches establish partitions between each pharyngeal arch. Pharyngeal pouches form between the arches on the endodermal side, while pharyngeal grooves (or clefts) form between the arches on the lateral ectodermal surface of the neck area. The pouches line up with the clefts in fish, and the thin segments that form gills. In mammals and birds, there are six pharyngeal pouches. The fifth and sixth pharyngeal pouches are contained within the fourth pharyngeal pouch, giving humans four pharyngeal pouches.

9. these are small pouches or sacs in the lungs where exchange of carbon dioxide and oxygen takes place​


Answer:

ALVEOLI are the very small air sacs where the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide takes place. CAPILLARIES are blood vessels in the walls of the alveoli. Blood passes through the capillaries, entering through your PULMONARY ARTERY and leaving via your PULMONARY VEIN.

Answer:

ALVEOLI

Explanation:

ALVEOLI are the very small air sacs where the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide takes place.

CAPILLARIES are blood vessels in the walls of the alveoli. Blood passes through the capillaries, entering through your PULMONARY ARTERY and leaving via your PULMONARY VEIN. While in the capillaries, blood gives off carbon dioxide through the capillary wall into the alveoli and takes up oxygen from air in the alveoli.
