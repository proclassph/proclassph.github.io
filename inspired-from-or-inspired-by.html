Answer:

Landscape

Although paintings from the earliest ancient and Classical periods included natural scenic elements, landscape as an independent genre did not emerge in the Western tradition until the Renaissance in the 16th century. In the Eastern tradition, the genre can be traced back to 4th-century-ce China.

Orgami

Many studies assert that origami was invented by the Japanese about a thousand years ago, but its roots may well be in China. It is also highly probable that the process of folding was applied to other materials before paper was invented, so the origins of recreational folding may lie with cloth or leather.

Chinese kite

The kite is believed to have originated in China. Since its invention, there have been many adaptations to the kite by various cultures around the world

Korean mask

The masks originated in the Hahoe Folk Village and Byeongsan Village, North Gyeongsang Province, South Korea. They are counted among the treasures of South Korea, and the oldest Hahoe mask is on display in the National Museum of Korea.

Woodblock

Woodblock printing or block printing is a technique for printing text, images or patterns used widely throughout East Asia and originating in China in antiquity as a method of printing on textiles and later paper. As a method of printing on cloth, the earliest surviving examples from China date to before 220 AD.

12. Invention(s) inspired by Cartesian Coordinates or by inspired from the work of René DescartesWrite about the invention, Who is the proponent, his biography..​


Answer:

1. Graphical Calculator: The graphical calculator was invented by the French mathematician and philosopher René Descartes in 1637. Descartes developed the Cartesian coordinate system, which uses the coordinates x and y to represent points in a plane. This system allows for a graphical representation of equations and curves, making it easier to visualize mathematical problems. The graphical calculator is a device that was created to take advantage of the Cartesian coordinate system and allow users to quickly and easily plot equations, graphs, and data points. The graphical calculator is now a standard tool in mathematics and science, used by students and professionals alike.

2. Cartesian Robot: The Cartesian robot was developed in the 1950s, inspired by the work of René Descartes. The Cartesian robot is a type of robot that uses the Cartesian coordinate system to move around in three-dimensional space. This type of robot is ideal for tasks that require precise and repeatable motion, such as welding, painting, and assembly. Cartesian robots are now commonly used in factories and other industrial settings, and have even been adapted for use in medical and surgical applications.
