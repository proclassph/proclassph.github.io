9. In a recent study, researchers found a negative correlation between income levels and the incidence of drop outs in the bscs course. jim thinks that this means that being poor leads to poor performance in school. is he correct in his conclusion


Answer:

A negative correlation means that as one variable increases, the other variable decreases. In this case, two instances may happen if there is a negative correlation:

a. High income, low incidence of dropouts

b. Low income, high incidence of dropouts

Based on this relationship, we can assume that being poor leads to poor performance in school. Being poor means that students:

1. may not get the necessities they need for school, like food and school materials or in other words support and;

2. opt to drop out to find work to support themselves or their family.

Therefore, yes. Jim may be correct in his conclusion. But other factors should be explored too.

It was found the population has a standard deviation of 5 and the analyst wanted 95% confidence level. what is the length of the confidence internal of the population if the population of players is 1441.​


Answer:

To find the length of the confidence interval for the population mean scoring capacities in a sepak takraw tournament with a 95% confidence level and a population standard deviation of 5, we need to use the formula:

CI = Z* (σ/√n)

where:

CI = confidence interval

Z = the z-score for the desired confidence level (in this case, 95% confidence level corresponds to a z-score of 1.96)

σ = population standard deviation (given as 5)

n = sample size (in this case, the population size is given as 1441, so we can assume that the entire population is the sample)

Substituting the given values into the formula, we get:

CI = 1.96 * (5 / √1441)

CI = 1.96 * (5 / 37.92)

CI = 0.256

Therefore, the length of the confidence interval for the population mean scoring capacities is 0.256. This means that we can be 95% confident that the true population mean scoring capacities lie within 0.256 points of the sample mean scoring capacities.
