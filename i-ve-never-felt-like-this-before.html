24. what is the result i felt like a hero?​


Answer:

what is felt like a hero?

Hero at work” is not the appropriate way to describe my feeling when this situation hapened. I had to resolve it my own way and it did some good all round. My feeling was “Satisfaction".

After the 1971 war I as a major was posted to the AHQ in the Branch that dealt with officers’ postings and promotions. I was dealing with policy matters related to these issues. There was an Army Order that was like Gospel for our Policy Section.(Army Orders, AO, are issued under the directions of Army Chief and copies are available in all units).

My superintendent brought to me the AO to read and become familiar with it. It was to be my bread and butter here. There was one instruction in this AO which specifically stated that high average (HA) and above average (AA) officers' had to be recommended for promotion to the next rank. I was dealing with reports on Lt Cols, Majors and below only. Those days selection process started from Majors’ rank.

Inspite of the AO, I found that majority of reports were going wrong on this. Even senior officers (Generals) writing reports were not recommending people for promotion for the next rank even after AA or HA awards in the numerical assessment. This was contrary to our AO and unfair to officers. It had to be corrected.

Being new in the job, I asked my Superintendent for his opinion on solving the problem. He was an old hand and his views on the issue couldn't be ignored. He said that we could not address each and every discrepancy with the erring formation (throght the chain) because of sheer volume of paperwork it would generate; and we didn't have the staff to handle it. At best we could handle nearly 30 percent and get them corrected.

The problem was huge. It had a moral angle too.

It was a patently unfair situation for the remainder affected people. I impressed upon our superintendent that our inaction was not going to affect just the officer. Each affected officer would have 5 or 6 other people of his immediate family whose lives were to be adversely affected by our inaction (forced upon us). It was not going to be fair to them too. He conceded that he hadn't thought of it from this angle.

Getting all reports to comply with the AO was imperative as these were soon to be digitalised. It meant that promotion and appointments' panels were to be generated by newly acquired computer. Postings/Promotions were to be made based on the computer outputs with some manual intervention.

After hearing out my superintendent, I began to churn the problem in my head. It went on for a few days.

I had got 50 flawed reports of Majors and Lt Cols brought to me. Going through each with a fine tooth comb, it was possible to identify the mistakes made .by the reported officer and by all in the reporting chain. This list had 25 + items.

Cyclostyled letter was made listing all these mistakes. Addresses were 4 command HQs. My name underneath was also inserted to keep typing effort for each letter to zero. I had to only sign the letter and tick the error that needed to be corrected and draw a line cancelling out the rest. Within a couple of weeks all my staff became familiar with our new method. It began to work flawlessly for 100 percent cases that needed attention.

Recently I was told by a serving brigadier of my Regiment that the above procedure is still in vogue after nearly 48 years.
