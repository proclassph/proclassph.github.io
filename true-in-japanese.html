22. True or False: Movies were not rampant during the Japanese era.


The answer is TRUE if you are referring about the Japanese occupation in the Philippines.

Film-making in the Philippines during the Japanese occupation in World War II was put to a sudden halt. The Japanese confiscated all film equipment at that time. They only use film-making for their propaganda at that time. Filipino movie actors and actresses could no longer appear in films. Almost all actors depended only on stage shows on most movie theaters. Live theater began to thrive again and replaced film cinemas. Filipino stage plays such as the comedia, zarzuela, and bodabil still remained in the country as forms of entertainment and expressions.

Long before the Japanese occupations began, Japanese are enjoying their traditional stage plays or theater acts. The most popular form of theater in Japan are the following:

Kabuki Noh and Kyogen Bunraku
