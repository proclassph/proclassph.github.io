11. what does collision mean?A an act i stance evading B. an act or instance of colliding C. an act instance of avoidance D. an act or instance of prevention​


        Option B is the correct answer because collision refers to an act or instance of two objects coming into contact with each other with forceful impact. The word "collision" is derived from the Latin word "collidere," which means "to strike together." In contrast, options A, C, and D are incorrect because they refer to actions that involve avoiding, evading, or preventing collisions, which is not the definition of the word "collision." Therefore, the answer B correctly reflects the meaning of the term "collision."
