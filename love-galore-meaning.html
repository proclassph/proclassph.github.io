3. TASK 10 PURPOSE GALORE!


The author’s purpose is their intention of writing and how it should be delivered to the readers. Authors may persuade, inform and entertain.

1. When the author thought of giving pamphlets to advise people that eating animals, or products from animals, is cruel and unnecessary, his purpose is to persuade.

2. The purpose of the author in this book is to inform since it’s a textbook about arts. He wants to deliver the proper way of doing crafts.

3. The author is giving the readers the purpose of entertainment, since the book consists of random stories, could be fictional or narrative.

4. Since it’s about a speech of a politician, he is campaigning quality education and medical assistance to the children in far towns. So the author is persuading.

5. The author wants to give information about the wonder drug that could heal dengue; the author is informing.

6. The author’s purpose is to inform since it’s about the comparison and differences of the economic policies of the Philippines and Vietnam.

7. The novel ‘Romeo and Juliet’ is a fictional story, so Shakespeare’s purpose was to entertain the readers with this tragic love story.

8. The purpose of publishing an announcement on a website about local dog ordinance is to persuade the readers.
