RATIONAL ALGEBRAIC EXPRESSIONS VS. NOT RATIONAL ALGEBRAIC EXPRESSIONS

The following equations fall under the rational algebraic expressions:

[tex]\frac{y+2}{y-2}[/tex][tex]\frac{a}{y^2-x^9}[/tex][tex]\frac{c}{a-2}[/tex][tex]\frac{1}{a^6}[/tex][tex]\frac{1-m}{m^3}[/tex][tex]\frac{k}{3k^2-6k}[/tex]

On the other hand, the following equations fall under the irrational algebraic expressions category:

[tex]\frac{m+2}{\sqrt{2x} }[/tex][tex]\frac{c^4}{\sqrt[8]{5a} }[/tex]

What is an algebraic expression?

An algebraic expression is a type of expression made up of variables, coefficients, constants, and algebraic operations. These expressions are made up of terms or polynomials.

When does it become rational or irrational?

It can be considered rational when both the numerator and the denominator are polynomials.As for the irrational category, a number is said to be irrational when it cannot be expressed as a fraction or does not have terminating or repeating decimals. Polynomials written under a radical sign are irrational algebraic functions.
