10. Nathi, Paul, and Tim worked in Mr. Setati's garden. Nathi worked for 5 hours, Paul for 4 hours and Tim for 3 hours. Mr. Setati gave the boys a total of R600 for their work. How should they divide the R 600 among the three of them?​


Answer:

To divide the R600 among the three boys, we need to find the total number of hours worked and then divide the total amount of money by the total number of hours worked to find the hourly rate. Each boy will then receive their hourly rate multiplied by the number of hours they worked.

Total hours worked = 5 + 4 + 3 = 12

Hourly rate = R600 ÷ 12 hours = R50 per hour

Nathi's pay = 5 hours x R50 per hour = R250

Paul's pay = 4 hours x R50 per hour = R200

Tim's pay = 3 hours x R50 per hour = R150

Therefore, Nathi should receive R250, Paul should receive R200, and Tim should receive R150

20. Mark and paul painted a fence in 4 hours, paul painted a fence by himself last year, the year before, mark painted it by himself, but he took 6 hours longer than paul. How long did each of them take to finish painting alone?​


Answer:

[tex]\boxed{\texttt{Paul: 6 hours}} [/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\texttt{Mark: 12 hours}}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Formula:

[tex]\mathtt{r=\dfrac{1}{t}}[/tex]

where:

r = rate

t = time

For Paul:

t₁ = x

[tex]\mathtt{r_{1}=\dfrac{1}{x}}[/tex]

For Mark:

Since Paul can paint the fence 6 hours faster than Mark, we can write t₂ as:

t₂ = x + 6

[tex]\mathtt{r_{2}=\dfrac{1}{x+6}}[/tex]

If Mark and Paul work together:

t₁₂ = 4

[tex]\mathtt{r_{12}=\dfrac{1}{4}}[/tex]

The sum of r₁ and r₂ is r₁₂

[tex]\mathtt{r_{1} \: + \: r_{2} \: = \: r_{12}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{1}{x} \: + \: \dfrac{1}{x+6} \: = \: \dfrac{1}{4}}[/tex]

Solving for x,

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{1}{x} \: + \: \dfrac{1}{x+6} \: = \: \dfrac{1}{4}} \longleftarrow \: \texttt{multiply both sides by 4x(x+6)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{4[(x+6)+x]=x(x+6)} \longleftarrow \: \texttt{simplify and combine like terms}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{8x+24=x^{2}+6x}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{x^{2}-2x-24=0} \longleftarrow \: \texttt{factor}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{(x-6)(x+4)=0}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{x_{1}=6 \: and \: x_{2}=-4}[/tex]

[tex]\texttt{The most logical value for x is 6}[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] [tex]\mathtt{x=6}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{x+6=12}[/tex]

[tex]\texttt{Paul took \boxed{\texttt{6 hours}} to paint the fence alone.}[/tex]

[tex]\texttt{Mark took \boxed{\texttt{12 hours}} to paint the fence alone.}[/tex]
