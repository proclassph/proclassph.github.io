This is because a more massive car will have more inertia, and will therefore take longer to come to a stop. The stopping distance will also increase as the coefficient of friction between the car and the surface it is traveling on decreases. This is because a lower coefficient of friction will mean that there is less force acting to slow the car down. This is because a faster car will have more kinetic energy, and will therefore take longer to come to a stop.
