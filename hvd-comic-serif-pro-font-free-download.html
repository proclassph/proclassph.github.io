2. how will you differentiate serif fonts and sans serif fonts?​


Answer:

hehe

Explanation:

The answer is simply in the name. A serif is a decorative stroke that finishes off the end of a letters stem (sometimes also called the “feet” of the letters). In turn, a serif font is a font that has serifs, while a sans serif is a font that does not (hence the “sans”).


3. What font is best suited for the contents of a printed book?A Serif fontB. Sans Serif fontC. Decorative fontD. All of the choices​


Answer:

A. Serif font

Step-by-step explanation:

Serif fonts help with readability, and are therefore preferable in the body of a book. The “serif” is the decorative stroke that finishes each end of a letter (think Times Roman). Serif fonts are easier on the reader's eye than sans-serif fonts; the stroke leads the reader's eye from one letter to the next.
