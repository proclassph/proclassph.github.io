17. The Sun by John Galsworthy Exposition — Inciting incident — Raising Action — Climax — Falling Action — Denouement​


Answer:

Sun Story Structure.

Exposition:

"The Sun" by John Galsworthy is a short story that portrays the life of a young boy named Harold, who lives with his parents in a small village. The story describes the daily routine of Harold's life and his interactions with the people around him.

Inciting incident:

The inciting incident in the story occurs when Harold's father, who is a farmer, dies unexpectedly. This event changes Harold's life dramatically as he is forced to take on more responsibility and become the head of the household.

Rising action:

The rising action in the story follows Harold's struggles as he tries to cope with the loss of his father and take care of his family. He faces financial difficulties and must work hard to make ends meet. Harold also develops a close relationship with a girl named Edie, who is the daughter of a wealthy neighbor.

Climax:

The climax of the story occurs when Harold's mother becomes seriously ill, and he is forced to make a difficult decision. He must choose between using the money he has saved to marry Edie or using it to pay for his mother's medical treatment.

Falling action:

The falling action in the story follows Harold's decision and the consequences of his actions. Harold chooses to use the money for his mother's treatment, which leads to the end of his relationship with Edie.

Denouement:

The denouement of the story occurs when Harold's mother recovers from her illness, and Harold is able to find a new sense of purpose in life. He realizes that his responsibilities to his family are more important than his personal desires, and he begins to find happiness in taking care of them.
