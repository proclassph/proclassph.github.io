24. Choose a one prophet and how he/she became a prophet and what he accomplished and what is your reflection. ​


Answer:
One prophet I would like to talk about is Moses. According to the Bible, Moses was born in Egypt during a time when the Pharaoh ordered the death of all male Hebrew babies. His mother put him in a basket and sent him down the river, where he was found and raised by the Pharaoh's daughter. As an adult, Moses saw the mistreatment of his people and was called by God to lead them out of Egypt and into the Promised Land.

Moses accomplished many things, including freeing the Hebrew people from slavery and leading them through the wilderness for forty years. He received the Ten Commandments from God and delivered them to the people, establishing the basis for Jewish law and morality. Moses also established the Tabernacle, a portable tent that served as a place of worship and symbolized the presence of God among his people.

In my reflection, I think Moses' story is a powerful example of how God can use ordinary people to accomplish extraordinary things. Moses was not perfect - he doubted himself and argued with God at times - but he ultimately trusted in God's plan and led his people to freedom. His story reminds us that no matter how unlikely or inadequate we may feel, God can use us to do great things if we are willing to trust and obey him.
