2. Based on your understanding, describe the role of city/provincial prosecutors and private prosecutors in the Criminal Justice process.​


Answer:

Explanation:

The National Prosecution Service has two roles in the Criminal Justice System: (1) to investigate complaints of criminal activity, and (2) to prosecute all cases involving breaches of penal laws. It's a quasi-judicial body tasked with ensuring that laws are carried out fairly.

When only the civil liability is being handled by a private lawyer, the director of the prosecutors office must give a written authority to the private prosecutor to try the case even though the public prosecutor is not present. According to Sec. 5, Rule 110, the written authority must be sent to the court prior to the private prosecutor's presentation of evidence. With this authority on file, the court will schedule the case's trial, as well as other cases prosecuted by private lawyers with assigned authority, on different days, without the need for the public prosecutor's involvement.

5. state the differences and similarities of a public prosecutor and a private prosecutor. Provide atleast 5 each​


Answer:

Public prosecutors and private prosecutors are both involved in the legal system, but they have different roles and responsibilities. Here are the differences and similarities between them:

Differences:

Employment: Public prosecutors are employed by the government, while private prosecutors are hired by individuals or organizations.

Scope of authority: Public prosecutors have the authority to handle criminal cases that are brought by the government, while private prosecutors can only represent clients in cases they are hired for.

Power of investigation: Public prosecutors have the power to investigate and gather evidence, while private prosecutors do not have this authority.

Accountability: Public prosecutors are accountable to the government and must follow certain guidelines and regulations, while private prosecutors have more flexibility in how they approach cases.

Salary: Public prosecutors receive a salary from the government, while private prosecutors are paid by their clients.

Similarities:

Education and training: Both public and private prosecutors must have a degree in law and have completed a bar exam.

Representation: Both types of prosecutors represent individuals or organizations in court.

Role in the legal system: Both public and private prosecutors play a crucial role in the legal system and uphold the law.

Legal knowledge: Both types of prosecutors have a deep understanding of the legal system and its processes.

Responsibility: Both public and private prosecutors are responsible for presenting evidence and making arguments in court on behalf of their clients.

13. What is the job of a Prosecutor?


Answer:

Prosecutors shall perform an active role in criminal proceedings, including institution of prosecution and, where authorized by law or consistent with local practice, in the investigation of crime, supervision over the legality of these investigations, supervision of the execution of court decisions and the exercise of other functions as representatives of the public interest.


14. Andres is a Vice Mayor of the Municipality of Pantukan. He was charged with the crime of rape before the office of the OMBUDSMAN. Accordingly, he maliciously raped his secretary at his office during office hours. The vice mayor moved to quash the complaint because according to him it is the office of the prosecutor or Department of Justice that has jurisdiction to investigate of crime as charged. Is the Vice Mayor correct?


Answer:

No, the Vice Mayor is not correct. The Office of the Ombudsman has jurisdiction to investigate and prosecute cases involving public officials, including crimes committed by them in the performance of their official duties or in relation to their office. This includes cases of rape committed by a public official, such as the Vice Mayor.

It is important to note that the Office of the Ombudsman is a separate and independent office from the Department of Justice, and has its own mandate and jurisdiction to investigate and prosecute cases involving public officials. The Vice Mayor cannot use his position as an excuse to evade investigation and prosecution for the crime of rape, and his motion to quash the complaint on the basis of jurisdiction would likely be denied.
