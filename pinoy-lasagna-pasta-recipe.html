4. Daisy is cooking pasta. the recipe says she needs 480 grams of pasta for 4 people. how many kilograms will she need for 14


Answer:

1.68 kilograms


Step-by-step explanation:

Proportion:

480:4 = n:14


Solve for n:

Change ":" sign to "/" sign

480/4 = n/14

Multiply both sides by 14

6720/4 = n

1680 = n


For 14 people, she need 1680 grams of pasta

Unit must be expressed in kilograms


Conversion:

1 kilogram = 1000 grams

1680 g (1 kg / 1000g)

= 1.68 kg

13. maghanap ng isang recipe sa internet ng pagkaing pinoy sinigang na bangus​


Answer:

Sinigang Na Bangus Recipe

This is a recipe for sinigang na bangus

Course Fish

Cuisine FilipinoPrep Time10 minutes

Cook Time30 minutes

Total Time 40 minutes

Servings 3

Author Vanjo Merano

Ingredients

1. 2 lbs. bangus milkfish, cleaned and sliced

2. 1 40g pack Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix (Original)

3. 1 bunch fresh kangkong leaves

4. 12 pieces sitaw snake beans, cut into 2 inch pieces

5. 6 to 8 pieces okra

6. 2 pieces long green pepper siling pansigang

7. 1 medium tomato wedged

8. 1 medium yellow onion wedged

9. 2 ½ tablespoons fish sauce

10. ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper

11. 2 quarts water

Instructions

1. Heat a cooking pot. Pour water into it.

2. Add tomato and onion. Let boil

3. Add bangus. Cover and cook in medium heat between 8 to 12 minutes.

4.Add knorr sinigang sa sampaloc mix. Stir cook for 2 minutes.

5. Put the long green pepper into the pot. Add snake beans and okra. Stir cover and cook for 5 to 7 minutes.

6. Add fish sauce and ground black pepper stir

7. Put the kangkomg leaves into the pot. Cover the pot and turn the heat off. Let it stay in 5 minutes.

8. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.

9. Share and enjoy

Nutrition

Serving 3g

22. what is the significant contribution of following the recipe in preparing pasta​


Answer:

Timing the cooking of the pasta to finish as the sauce and the rest of the meal is ready helps assure it is perfectly cooked. You don't want pasta sitting and sticking together while you are trying to get the rest of a meal finished.
