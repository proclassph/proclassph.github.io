3. The common colors tones found in a Bagobo's t'nalak. A. black and red C. orange, brown and black B. deep brown and blood-red D. red, brown and black

The correct answer is letter D. Red,brown and black. • T'nalak is a traditional Mindanao fabric made by the T'bolis, Tboli people of Lake Sebu, South Cotabato. This hand-woven traditional fabric is made of Abaca fibers and comes in three colors: red, black, and the natural color of the Abaca leaves. Colorants in the materials are naturally colored and boiled in with plant bark, roots, and leaves. It is a family tradition, and it is believed that the Tinalak's intricate and creative creations were seen in their dreams and inspired them to work. They can't plan a Tinalak until they've fantasized about it. They are also known as "Dream Weavers." The T'nalak fabric is highly useful in the T'boli culture. It can also be found at big life events such as puberty, marriage, and death. It is the medium that sanctifies these rites, enveloping them in the length of its fabric like a benediction. It's also been dubbed "woven dreams." It is one of these stuff and more. The T'nalak worked as both literature and poetry in a culture that lacked a written language. The T'bolis revealed something about themselves in the T'nalak: their dreams, beliefs, legends, and even their religion. T'bolis weave the true and metaphysical in T'nalak abaca threads using assorted geometrical forms and the trademark red, black, and white colors. Furthermore, the weaving method incorporates the personal, social, and spiritual. When a weaver reaches a certain degree of ability, she is known as a "master weaver"–someone who can interpret and derive inspiration from dreams, hence the term "dreamweavers." " According to all sources, this seems to be a very personal experience for the weaver, and if she succeeds, she becomes an artist." It is also social in the way that the T'nalak binds all the T'boli believe in. All of the elements that make up T'boli social life are gathered in the T'nalak by the weaver's talent. Finally, it is linguistic in the way that other tribes use it to categorize T'bolis, and the T'nalak is uniquely and distinctly T'boli. Tinalak are also highly valued possessions during marriage, as well as the covering for childbirth to ensure safe delivery and for sale. When they sell their artwork, they encircle it with a brass ring as a plea to the spirits to make them or please them. It is assumed that cutting the cloth would cause sickness.

19. If sunlight is passed through a prism, and then a deep red rose is placed in various parts of the spectrum, the rose will appear brown or black in all regions except in red the red region. In the red part of the spectrum, the petals appear red but the green leaves and stem will appear black. When the rose is held in a white light, the petals appear red and the leaves and stem green. Why?


When white light such as the sunlight passes through a prism, the light is 'bent' towards a different angle. Remember however, that white light is a mixture of different frequencies/wavelengths of light, and each of these bend differently inside the prism, which results to us seeing a spectrum of different colors.
Seeing colors means that the object we're looking at only reflect colors of that specific wavelength. This is why when we look at something 'red', that object only reflects the frequency of red light best.
Now going back to the experiment. If we try to look at the bright red rose under a spectrum of light, we know that this object only reflects the spectrum of red light. Under, for example, blue light, our eyes will see two frequencies coming in and translate it as a kind of superposition of two frequencies, which leads us to think we're seeing black or brown. 
This similar explanation also applies to why the petal looks red under the red light, and the leaf and stem black. Under the red light, the color of the petal that our eyes interpret coincides, and makes us 'see' red. However, the green color adds to seeing red, which causes us to not see the original green color.
